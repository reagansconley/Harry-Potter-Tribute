Hello, Muggles!

I'm Harry Potter, and I am an 8th Year wizard at the Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. My most recent accomplishment is probably defeating Lord Voldemort in the Battle of Hogwarts.

A bit about me, then?

I learned I was a wizard on my eleventh birthday - On my eleventh birthday, I learned that I was a wizard from Rubeus Hagrid and began attending Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. I thought I was just an ordinary boy with no parents who lived in a cupboard before then.

At school, I became best friends with Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger. I later became the youngest Quidditch Seeker in over a century, and the captain of the Gryffindor House Quidditch Team in my sixth year (winning two Quidditch Cups!).

I became quite famous in my early years for protecting the Philosopher’s Stone from Voldemort (the Dark Lord who killed my parents), saving Ron’s sister Ginny Weasley, solving the mystery of the Chamber of Secrets, slaying Salazar Slytherin’s basilisk, and learning how to conjure a corporeal stag Patronus at the age of thirteen. I won the Triwizard Tournament in fourth year, although the competition ended with the tragic death of my classmate, Cedric Diggory, and the return of Voldemort. During fifth year, I reluctantly taught and led Dumbledore’s Army. I also fought in the Battle of the Department of Mysteries, during which I lost my beloved godfather, Sirius Black.

I played a significant role in many other battles of the Second Wizarding War. Ron, Hermione, and I hunted down and destroyed Voldemort’s Horcruxes. During the Battle of Hogwarts, I lost many friends and adopted family members (an unfair amount, really), and I nearly sacrificed myself when encountering Voldemort. I knew it was the only way to destroy the fragment of Voldemort’s soul inside of me. In limbo, however, I was given the choice to either live or move on, and I chose to live. Naturally. After I awoke, I confronted Voldemort and defeated him once and for all.

For my final year at Hogwarts, I am focused on NOT dying for once, playing Quidditch, hanging out with my friends, and pretending NOT to be head over heels for Draco Malfoy.

Books written about me.

J.K. Rowling, a Muggle author, documented all of my adventures and became quite famous herself.
